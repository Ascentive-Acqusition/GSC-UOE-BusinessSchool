Taking ASE

We are a two-person undergraduate team from the University of Exeter. Over the past two weeks we have analysed ASE's technology, market position and commercial infrastructure to identify where the biggest scaling opportunities sit.

Technology Lens
Product Development
A fast lane commercialising drag-reduction surfaces now, and a slow lane developing quantum algorithms and advanced materials in parallel, each funding and protecting the other.
Marketing Lens
Social and Marketing Infrastructure
A three-stage marketing funnel, rebuilt website, smart lead capture and phased paid distribution to create a commercial pipeline from nothing.
Recommendation 1 of 2: Technology Strategy
Phase 1: 0 to 12 Months
Two timelines running
simultaneously.
Fast Lane: Near Term
Drag-Reduction Adhesive Strips
The beachhead product. Already at TRL 5 to 6, close to commercial deployment. Delivers 5 to 50% drag reduction and 10 to 25% fuel savings, with peaks of up to 39% in testing. Existing solutions sit at around 0.8%. This is not a marginal gain. It is a step change. Marine first, because fuel consumption is high and regulation is low, meaning faster adoption and immediate cost savings for buyers.
TRL 5-6
Slow Lane: Long Term
Quantum Algorithms and Advanced Materials
Running in the background from day one. Advanced CFD, early-stage quantum simulation at TRL 2 to 3, and next-generation materials at TRL 3 to 4. Critically, this is externally funded through grants, so it does not compete with the fast lane for capital. It builds the long-term defensibility that prevents the technology being copied or made redundant once the fast lane generates revenue.
TRL 2-4
The fast lane works only with independent validation. The slow lane is what makes it defensible.
Wind tunnel testing, real-world trials and third-party certification are non-negotiable for the fast lane. Without them, there are no enterprise contracts and no licensing deals. ASE's 3 to 5 day design-to-testing cycle is the competitive advantage that keeps the fast lane ahead. Meanwhile the slow lane runs on external funding, building the quantum and materials platform that will protect revenue once competitors begin to copy. By month 12: validation complete, initial revenue in, marine market entry secured.
Recommendation 1 of 2: Technology Strategy
Phase 2: 12 to 48 Months
From product to platform.
Market expansion
Marine to Trucks to EVs
ASE expands from marine into trucks, logistics and eventually electric vehicles. The core surface technology is applied across each sector without redesigning from scratch. The same fast lane, broader reach.
Business model shift
Technology and IP Company
A major shift happens here. ASE does not act as an OEM. Instead it partners with OEMs, licenses its technology and collects royalties, avoiding the hassle of production entirely. High-margin, asset-light revenue at scale.
Staying ahead
Version 1 to Version 2
Copying will happen. The CEO knows this. So instead of trying to prevent it, ASE stays ahead through continuous iteration. Version 1 at TRL 6. Version 2 moves toward TRL 7. The 3 to 5 day design-to-testing cycle creates a moving target competitors cannot keep up with.
Technology side
CFD, AI and Optional Quantum Upside
ASE continues scaling through advanced CFD and AI. Quantum remains optional upside, progressing toward TRL 6 to 7 over time. Even if quantum is delayed, the business still grows. Classical systems are sufficient to maintain the competitive edge.
By years 3 to 4
Multi-industry. Licensing active. Profitable.
Multi-industry adoption secured. OEM licensing model activated. Strong IP advantage established. ASE strengthens execution internally through clearer structure, stronger leadership and improved commercial capability. A scalable, profitable business.
Tesla: Proven Analogue
Started with the Roadster: low volume, high margin, immediate revenue. Used that revenue to scale to Model S then Model 3. Ran batteries, AI and autonomy as parallel deep tech programmes throughout. Never stopped the long-term work while commercialising the near-term product.
ASE: Same Logic
Drag surfaces are the Roadster. Marine is the early market. As revenue scales, ASE transitions to a licensing platform, exactly as Tesla transitioned from niche product to global platform. Quantum and advanced materials are the batteries: running in parallel, ready when the hardware matures.

Phase 3: 48 Months and Beyond
From surface improvements
to full-stack platform.
Full integration
One Integrated System
Surface engineering, advanced materials and quantum algorithms combine into a single platform. Core technologies approach TRL 7 to 9, unlocking eligibility for non-dilutive government grants and large-scale institutional contracts.
Quantum position
Upside if Ready, Lead if Not
If quantum hardware is mature, it enhances performance further: faster simulation, better materials, deeper optimisation. If not, ASE still leads using classical systems. The algorithms are already written and waiting. Either way, ASE wins.
Value chain
System-Level Optimisation
ASE shifts from improving individual surfaces to optimising entire systems: vehicles, fleets and energy infrastructure. Not just reducing drag on a boat hull, but engineering the performance of the whole system around it.
Global scale
Licensing, OEM Partnerships, Global Expansion
ASE scales through licensing, partnerships with OEMs and expansion across industries. High-margin royalty revenues with minimal marginal cost per unit. Revenue in the hundreds of millions by Year 9. NASDAQ target by Year 10.
The destination
Not just a product company.
A global deep-tech platform.
That is how ASE scales. Not just fast, but credibly. Fast lane generates revenue today. Slow lane builds what competitors cannot replicate tomorrow.

Recommendation 2 of 2: Marketing and Social
The Awareness Gap
This is not a skill gap.
It is an awareness gap.

Think of a world-class surgeon with 30 years of experience, every major award in their field, published in every leading journal. But no website, no referrals system and no way for a patient to find them. The skill is not the problem. The visibility is.

ASE is that surgeon. An R&D 100 award. Forbes coverage. Validation from Sandia National Laboratories. And according to LinkedIn's 2024 B2B marketing benchmarks, over 75% of B2B buyers research a vendor digitally before making any contact. If ASE cannot be found, none of the science reaches the buyers who need it.

The gap is not what they have built. It is who knows about it.
You can have the best product in any market and still lose to a competitor with a weaker product and a stronger presence. Awareness is not optional in B2B. It is the prerequisite to everything else.
